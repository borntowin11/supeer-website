Understanding Accredited Courses

The National Universities Commission (NUC) and bodies like COREN (for engineering) accredit university programs to ensure quality. Accreditation status can change, so continuous verification is essential. A degree from an unaccredited program may not be recognized.

For a full list of accredited programs, visit the official NUC website: NUC Accredited Programmes.

General JAMB Cut-Off Marks & Considerations

Category Typical JAMB Score Range Notes
**JAMB General Minimum** 140 (Universities), 100 (Polytechnics/COEs) This is the *minimum* score set by JAMB to be considered for admission into any university. Actual university/departmental cut-offs are almost always higher.
**Less Competitive Courses** 140 - 180 Examples: Some Education courses, some Arts/Humanities courses, some Agricultural courses in less competitive universities.
**Moderately Competitive Courses** 180 - 220 Examples: Most Social Sciences, Management Sciences, some pure Sciences, and some Arts courses in average to good federal/state universities.
**Highly Competitive Courses (Federal/Top State Universities)** 220 - 280+ Examples: Medicine & Surgery, Law, Pharmacy, Nursing, Accounting, Mass Communication, all Engineering disciplines, Computer Science in top federal universities (e.g., UI, UNILAG, OAU, UNN, UNILORIN).
**Important Note** Varies Annually **Exact JAMB cut-off marks are determined by individual universities and departments each admission year.** Always refer to the official JAMB Brochure and the specific university's admission portal for the most accurate and up-to-date information for your desired year of admission. Post-UTME/screening scores also significantly contribute to final admission.

Essential O'Level Requirements

Most universities require credit passes in at least five (5) subjects, including English Language and Mathematics, obtained in not more than two sittings. However, highly competitive courses like Medicine often demand these credits in a single sitting, acting as a competitive filter.

Check the specific O'level requirements for your chosen course directly on the university's admission portal.

Navigating University Cut-Off Marks

While JAMB sets a general minimum cut-off, individual universities and courses set their own, often much higher, cut-off marks. These vary significantly based on competitiveness and demand, revealing a "competitive spectrum" among institutions. Aiming significantly higher than the minimum is advisable for competitive programs.

For the most current cut-off marks, always refer to the official university admission portals.
